*It seems rain's falling, it seems you're adrift.* That was basically my entire last week.

Two exams in two days, on its own not the end of the world, but everything around them had gone sideways too. *I had nights and I had days, beauty, pain, earth and the sky.* By the time I handed in the second paper I was running on pure spite™. *Waking up again, oh god, my mind is a mess.* I kept wishing for mornings that would repeatedly brush off the unplanned, unfinished, anxious nights, but they hadn't really come.

But it's Saturday now and both exams are done. I'm mostly free for the first time in a while, which is great except I'm still absolutely running on fumes. *A million thoughts already, phone turned off, calling out my wishes.*

I thought about just sleeping that afternoon. But I'd been spending too much time inside anyway, and lying in bed while burned out never helps, so I talked myself out of it and got up. Grabbed my bag and got on the bus, then the metro. *Let go of the bad thoughts, they'll hold me back. The metro will be my night, my hope.* Sleepy and tired, yes, but better sleepy and tired moving through the city than oozing on the bed staring at the ceiling.

Music on, phone off, notebook out, watching Hanoi go past the window. *Let me stay a while, stop the world from moving on,* while I'm in transit with nowhere to be. *No one will find me, no.* And then my brain finally gets quiet.

*Who are we to wonder where we're going? Who am I to tell me who I am?* The metro doesn't ask.

*Will it be morning soon? I was dreaming about a certain day.*

*It's so hard, so can we skip to the good part?* Can we? Please?

*But where's my beer and how far away is Võru?* 

I want something cold to drink, and Võru is extremely far from Hanoi. Man I miss it...

It's alright, I'm here now, Jarjar. I'll be there next time.
