I'm at that one famous and popular and whatever lake, the [Ho Guom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ho%C3%A0n_Ki%E1%BA%BFm_Lake). It was quite sunny and hot when I arrived at 4:30pm something, but now it's really chilly. 6:11pm at the time I started writing this. Hand-written first, because I promised myself I wouldn't pick up my phone on a whim, but I ended up doing just that earlier. ...And again just now, because I wanted to take a pic of this, lol. Man, I got to make not opening the phone so often a trait.

![But hey, at least I got this cool little image!](ho-guom-journal.png)

Anyway, the lake. This one lake is one of the most iconic lakes here, so suffice to say everyone around me is either a tourist with a selfie stick or families/couples going on a date. And then there's me, sitting alone on a wooden bench with a notebook, with a 1,5 liters bottle of water right by my side and a huge backpack just by the bench. I feel like an NPC already. The Turtle Tower is doing its thing and lights are on as I'm writing this.

I don't know why I came here today tbh. I needed somewhere that's not my room or my school because I've been sleeping all day too often, like 20 hours straight each day I don't have anything to do. How come, I honestly don't even know anymore, but if I had to guess then probably a combination of depression retreat and my April sleeping season. Anyway, I needed somewhere to go, so I hopped on random buses and looped all afternoon until I got bored. Dropped very close to the lake so why not ¯\\\_(ツ)_/¯

The lake itself is overrated, as with most touristy areas are, imo, because they're too crowded and hyped up. But gonna be real, when it gets chilly like this and the surrounding lights go orange, it seems not that bad actually. Fine, even.

It's 7pm now, so people are going for dinner. I'll stay here for a bit to enjoy the chill before the crowd comes back, and I'll be hopping on another bus I guess. Oh and a friend just hit me up to play, so guess I'm heading home anyway. Figured.
